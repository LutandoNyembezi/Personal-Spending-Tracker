# Personal-Spending-Tracker
This application helps people budget, one can even input spending for the car house etc - budget better and plan better for the months ahead.

Income tab — start by entering your monthly take-home pay and any other income sources. Everything auto-calculates as you type.
Spending tab — fill in your actual monthly costs across housing, transport, living, debt repayments, and savings. You can also add custom lines (like school fees, domestic worker, etc.) at the bottom.
Overview tab — once your numbers are in, this shows a breakdown by category with visual bars, a doughnut chart, and personalised budget insights (e.g. if your housing or debt costs are too high, or if you're not saving enough).
Forecast tab — plan 6 months ahead. You can set income growth, extra expected costs, a once-off big expense (like a holiday or appliance), and a monthly savings target. The chart shows your cumulative balance vs your target so you can see exactly when you'd run short.

The colour choices were very deliberate — a deep forest green accent that feels professional and trustworthy (not corporate or flashy), a warm off-white background so it's easy on the eyes for long sessions, and clear red/amber/green signals for money health that anyone can read at a glance regardless of background.

Saving a snapshot — once you've filled in your income and expenses for the month, go to the History tab, pick the month and year, and hit "Save snapshot." It stores everything in one click.
Exporting to CSV — you can export a single month or all months at once as a .csv file. Save that file to your phone's gallery, Google Drive, WhatsApp it to yourself, or put it on a USB — it's yours forever and opens in Excel or Google Sheets.
Importing back — if you're on a new device or come back years later, just hit "Import CSV" and upload your old file. All your history comes back instantly.
Looking back 2 years from now — every time you save a snapshot and export the CSV at month end, you'll build up a full history. Two years later you can open that spreadsheet and see exactly what you earned and spent in any month.
One important note: the snapshots stored in the browser will disappear if you clear your browser data, so always export the CSV as your permanent backup after saving each month.
